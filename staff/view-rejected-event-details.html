Rejected In-Person Open Event

International Food Festival

Multi-cultural food festival featuring international cuisines from student communities worldwide.

Rejection Reasons

Reviewed by: Dr. Admin Reviewer

October 8, 2025 at 4:15 PM

1

Scheduling Conflict with Major Event

The proposed date (October 25, 2025) conflicts directly with Homecoming Week activities, which is a major university-wide event. All venues and resources are allocated to Homecoming Week events during this period. This conflict cannot be resolved for the current semester.

2

Insufficient Preparation Time

The event submission was made only 2 weeks before the proposed date. Food festival events require minimum 6-8 weeks lead time for proper vendor coordination, health permits, safety inspections, and logistical arrangements. The current timeline does not allow adequate preparation to meet university safety and quality standards.

3

Missing Critical Safety Protocols

The proposal lacks detailed safety and sanitation protocols required for food handling events. Essential missing elements include:

  • Food safety and handling procedures
  • Health permit requirements for vendors
  • Sanitation and waste management plan
  • Allergen information and labeling protocols
  • Emergency response procedures

Administrator Recommendation: Consider resubmitting this event for the next semester (January-February 2026) with:

  • 6-8 weeks preparation time before the event date
  • Adjusted scope focusing on 5-10 cuisine booths initially
  • Complete safety, sanitation, and health protocols
  • Coordination with Student Life Office for proper planning
  • Budget breakdown including health permits and safety equipment

The concept is excellent and has strong potential. With proper planning time and documentation, this event could be very successful.
