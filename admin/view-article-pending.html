Great Dasmarinas Bay Clean-up Drive: A Community Success Story

Partnership Success Environmental Community

Article Summary

The recent coastal clean-up partnership between PCU-DASMA and Clean Seas Alliance exceeded all expectations, with 450 volunteers collecting 3.2 tons of waste and cleaning 1.8km of coastline. This successful initiative demonstrates the power of university-community collaboration.

Featured Image

Full Article Content

Last Saturday marked a significant milestone in environmental conservation as PCU-DASMA partnered with Clean Seas Alliance for a comprehensive coastal clean-up initiative at Dasmarinas Bay. The event brought together 450 volunteers, including students, faculty, staff, and community members, all united by a common goal: to restore and protect our coastal ecosystem.

Impressive Results

The initiative resulted in remarkable achievements: 3.2 tons of waste collected, 1.8 kilometers of coastline cleaned, and over 15 different types of recyclable materials sorted and properly disposed of. These numbers reflect not just the scale of the environmental challenge we face, but also the tremendous impact of coordinated community action.

Community Impact

"This partnership exemplifies what we can achieve when educational institutions and environmental organizations work hand-in-hand," said Dr. Maria Santos, PCU-DASMA's Environmental Studies Department Head. "Our students gained hands-on experience in environmental conservation while making a tangible difference in our community."

The event also included educational workshops on marine conservation, waste segregation, and sustainable practices, ensuring that participants left not only with cleaner beaches but also with knowledge to drive long-term change.
