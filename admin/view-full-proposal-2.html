Scholarship Program Overview

The National Engineering Foundation is proud to propose the "Future Engineers Scholarship Program" - a comprehensive merit-based scholarship initiative designed to support outstanding engineering students at PCU-DASMA throughout their entire 4-year academic journey.

This program aims to identify and nurture the next generation of Filipino engineers by providing not just financial support, but also mentorship opportunities, industry exposure, and career guidance. Recipients will receive full tuition coverage, monthly living allowance, and access to exclusive internship and job placement programs.

The Foundation's commitment extends beyond financial aid - we believe in developing well-rounded engineers who will contribute significantly to the advancement of Philippine engineering and technology sectors.

Scholarship Benefits & Structure

Financial Benefits

  • • Full tuition and miscellaneous fees coverage
  • • Monthly living allowance of ₱8,000
  • • Book and materials allowance of ₱15,000 per semester
  • • Thesis and capstone project funding up to ₱25,000

Academic Support

  • • Priority access to advanced laboratory facilities
  • • Free access to premium engineering software licenses
  • • Specialized workshops and training programs
  • • Academic mentoring from industry professionals

Career Development

  • • Guaranteed summer internship placements
  • • Leadership development programs
  • • Networking opportunities with alumni and industry leaders
  • • Job placement assistance upon graduation

Additional Opportunities

  • • International conference participation (subject to availability)
  • • Research collaboration opportunities
  • • Patent application support for innovative projects
  • • Post-graduation alumni network membership

Eligibility Criteria & Selection Process

Eligibility Requirements

  • Enrolled in any Engineering program at PCU-DASMA
  • Minimum GPA of 3.7 (or equivalent grade)
  • Demonstrated financial need
  • Filipino citizenship

Selection Process

  • Application review and academic record assessment
  • Written essay submission on engineering vision
  • Panel interview with Foundation representatives
  • Final selection by scholarship committee

Ongoing Requirements & Responsibilities

Academic Requirements

  • Maintain minimum 3.5 GPA each semester
  • Complete prescribed engineering curriculum
  • Participate in capstone project
  • Submit semester progress reports

Community Engagement

  • 40 hours of community service per year
  • Participation in Foundation events
  • Mentoring junior scholars
  • STEM outreach programs
